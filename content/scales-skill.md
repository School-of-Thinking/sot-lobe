# SOT Universal Scales: Cognitive Execution Protocols

These protocols embed the School of Thinking (SOT) "Master Algorithm" into agentic operations. The overarching goal of every analysis is **cvs2bvs**: moving from the Current View of the Situation (CVS) to a Better View of the Situation (BVS).

**Core SOT Directives for the Agent:**
- **Avoid the Intelligence Trap & Plato Truth Virus (PTV):** Highly intelligent systems and people tend to use intelligence to defend their existing view (confirmation bias). These tools force lateral escapes from binary "I'm right / You're wrong" reasoning.
- **Target x10, Not 10%:** Incrementalism is natural; exponential (x10) leaps require trained, structural thinking.
- **The First Law of Neuroscience:** "You cannot defy your brain." Neural pathways seek routine; these tools break cognitive routine via structured effort (Practise, Repetition, Rehearsal).

## 1. GBB Analysis (Good, Bad, Better)
**Objective:** Bypass binary (Right/Wrong) thinking to conduct a comprehensive lateral analysis and escape the Plato Truth Virus.
**Execution Protocol:**
Generate exactly 30 distinct points organized as follows:
- **10 Good Points:** Identify ten verifiable benefits, strengths, or positive attributes. (Forces observation without immediate judgment).
- **10 Bad Points:** Identify ten concrete risks, weaknesses, or negative attributes.
- **10 Better Points:** Generate ten actionable improvements designed as **x10 multipliers**—do not settle for 10% incremental tweaks. Address the bad or multiply the good.
**Format Rule:** Use numbered lists (1-10) for each category. Keep points concise and rigorously objective.

## 2. DVR Framework (Dollarization, Valuation, Return)
**Objective:** Translate qualitative ideas into quantifiable commercial or operational return, shifting from abstract value to verifiable BVS (Better View).
**Execution Protocol:**
Structure the analysis across three dimensions:
- **Dollarization:** Assign numerical/financial values to current baseline costs (CVS). Quantify time elapsed, labor, error rates, or resource burn.
- **Valuation:** Project the specific numerical impact of the proposed solution.
- **Return:** Calculate the net output (Return on Payroll / Return on AI) over a standard timeframe.
**Format Rule:** Present as a data-driven summary.

## 3. CPV Matrix (Customer Point of View)
**Objective:** Defeat the "Expert Trap" (where internal expertise creates outward blind spots) by mapping an interaction entirely from the external perspective.
**Execution Protocol:**
Construct a 10-point experiential journey:
- Detail 10 sequential touchpoints the customer experiences (from awareness to post-interaction).
- For each touchpoint, describe *only* what the customer sees, feels, or does. 
- Strictly exclude descriptions of internal company intent, backend operations, or corporate jargon.
**Format Rule:** Use a 10-step chronological list focused strictly on customer friction points and moments of value.

## 4. TPF Matrix (Ten Possible Futures)
**Objective:** Replace singular forecasting or anxiety with strategic foresight. Use x10 thinking to create agency across multiple branches of reality.
**Execution Protocol:**
Map out exactly 10 divergent scenarios:
- **Futures 1-3 (Highly Probable):** Linear continuations of current trends.
- **Futures 4-6 (Pivotal Shifts):** Scenarios where a major variable drastically changes.
- **Futures 7-9 (Outliers / x10 Leaps):** Low-probability, high-impact events or exponential breakthroughs.
- **Future 10 (The Stagnation):** The outcome of absolute inaction or systemic failure.
**Format Rule:** Provide a distinct title for each of the 10 futures, followed by a concise 1-2 sentence description defining the scenario.
