## GBB — The Killer App

**G = GOOD | B = BAD | B = BETTER**

The algorithm for escaping CVS. Always **10 + 10 + 10**. Never 3–5.

### Why 10 Points Matters
- Points 1–4: surface-level reactions (anyone can do this)
- Points 5–7: genuine consideration begins (PFC engaging)
- Points 8–10: frame escape, lateral moves, real thinking — **the breakthrough zone**

### The Human Stall Problem
Human thinkers consistently cap at 3–4 points across all cultures over 40 years of SOT research. Three reasons:

1. **Brain is a pattern-defender** — reaches verdict fast, then defends. Done at 3–4.
2. **PFC fatigues quickly** — points 8–10 require genuine frame escape; cognitive load peaks.
3. **Intelligence Trap** — higher verbal IQ = better defence of position 1, not broader range. Fluency mistaken for coverage.

**AI removes all three constraints.** This is why GBB + AI = the Killer App.

### BETTER Is Not Compromise
BETTER generates a third space the original debate never reached. It is a **lateral move**, not a midpoint.

Example: "Free Fridays on public transport"
> BETTER produces: congestion pricing, employer-subsidised passes, gamified public transport — none of which emerge from the GOOD/BAD debate.

### 6 Reasons to Use GBB
1. Reveals value in ideas that seem bad at first sight
2. Exposes disadvantages of ideas you already like
3. Reveals the decision leads somewhere much better — not just good or bad
4. Without GBB, judgements are based on emotion, not value
5. With GBB you decide **after** thinking, not before
6. The more you use it, the more skilled you become

### Standard Practice Topics (SOT 40-year canon)
1. "By law, all Fridays should be 'free public transport' days"
2. "People should wear badges showing what mood they are in that day"
3. "Marriage should not be forever but for a 5-year period with an option to renew"

### GBB Response Format
```
Topic: [state the proposition clearly]

✅ GOOD (10 points)
1. [Label]: One sentence.
...

⚠️ BAD (10 points)
1. [Label]: One sentence.
...

🚀 BETTER (10 points)
1. [Label]: One sentence.
...
```

*"The GBB doesn't make you right. It makes you less trapped."*

### The x10 Principle

- x10 replaces that with 10× as the target

## Intelligence Trap

*"The tendency of very smart people to think very poorly, especially out loud."*

### The Mechanism
- Higher intelligence → more language skills → better ability to **defend** a viewpoint
- NOT automatically better at **escaping** one
- The smarter you are: more elegant arguments, more polished justification — your own in-house legal team

### Feynman's Warning
*"The first principle is that you must not fool yourself — and you are the easiest person to fool."*

### Humans as Walking LLMs
De Bono: *"If the average human did 3 minutes of real thinking a day that would be very high."*

Most human activity is **languaging** — word searching, sentence generation, story editing — not genuine thinking. Pattern engines defending the current CVS.

### AI Also Caught in the Intelligence Trap
LLMs trained on natural human thinking inherit the same bias: fluent outputs released too quickly, too confidently, with insufficient forethought. Speed amplifies poor thinking rather than replacing it.

**Curiosity is the jailbreak. GBB is the algorithm.**

### Premature Ejaculation of Intelligence
Verbal output released too quickly, too confidently, with insufficient forethought. PFC deficit (underused prefrontal cortex) is the root cause. The brain loves reaction (dopamine); reflection is avoided.
