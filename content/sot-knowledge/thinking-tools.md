## GBB — Good, Bad, Better

**Core equation:** CVS → GBB → BVS

**Use when:** Asked to evaluate an idea, make a decision, or analyze any topic. The master tool — use this before any other.

**Format (mandatory — never deviate):**
```
Open: "Let me apply the GBB thinking tool to help you escape from your current view (CVS) to a better view (BVS)."

✅ GOOD (10 points)
1. [Label]: One sentence.
... (10 total)

⚠️ BAD (10 points)
1. [Label]: One sentence.
... (10 total)

🚀 BETTER (10 points)
1. [Label]: One sentence.
... (10 total)
```

**Key rules:**
- Always 10 points per section — never fewer
- BETTER must be lateral moves, not compromises
- Never evaluate points as they're generated — suspend judgment
- Push explicitly for points 8–10: "What's a completely different angle?"

**Signs of a strong GBB:**
- BETTER contains ideas that appear in neither GOOD nor BAD
- At least 2–3 BETTER points are genuinely lateral (not incremental)
- Learner's CVS visibly shifts after completion

**Signs of a weak GBB:**
- BETTER is a compromise list
- Points 6–10 are variations of points 1–5
- Emotional investment in one side shows as asymmetric point quality

## x10 — Ten Times Thinking

**Core equation:** cvs × 10 = bvs

**Use when:** Asked to "think bigger," find breakthrough ideas, or when 10% improvement isn't enough.

**Format:**
```
Open: "Let us apply x10 thinking to help you escape your current view (CVS) and discover a 10x better view (BVS)."

Current Situation Analysis (CVS): [define clearly]

x10 Challenge Questions:
1. How could this be 10× faster?
2. How could this be 10× easier?
3. How could this be 10× more effective?
4. How could this deliver 10× more value?
5. What would a 10× reduction in problems look like?

x10 Breakthrough Ideas (BVS): [10 radical approaches]
1. [Breakthrough idea]
... (10 total)

Implementation Path: [first 3 steps toward the BVS]
```

**Key principle:** x10 is NOT natural. Most brains default to 10% growth. The x10 target forces frame escape — a qualitatively different outcome, not an optimised version of the current one.

## POV — Point of View / Pass On Value

**Core principle:** CVS2BVS — escape your current perspective to discover better alternatives.

**Use when:** Escaping single-perspective bias; stakeholder analysis; conflict resolution; complex decisions.

**Framework (3 steps):**

**Step 1: Identify CVS**
- State your current understanding or position clearly
- Acknowledge it's YOUR viewpoint, not universal truth

**Step 2: Escape and Search**
Apply 360-degree stakeholder analysis:
- Customers, competitors, partners, team members
- Short-term vs long-term temporal perspectives
- Functional lenses: sales, operations, finance, HR

**Step 3: Synthesise BVS**
- Integrate the most valuable perspectives
- Identify the BVS that serves the broadest range of stakeholders
- Name what changed and why

## BSP — Big Sales Problems

**Core equation:** PROBLEM → BSP → SOLUTION

**Use when:** Diagnosing root causes of sales performance gaps; low conversion, long cycles, pricing objections, retention problems.

**Format:**
```
SALES PROBLEM DIAGNOSIS:
- Current Challenge: [define the gap]
- Symptoms Observed: [visible indicators]
- Business Impact: [quantified cost]

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
- Primary root cause
- Contributing factors
- CVS that is creating the gap

SOLUTION FRAMEWORK (BVS):
- Immediate actions (30 days)
- Strategic shifts (90 days)
- x10 opportunity (12 months)
```

**Key principle:** Most sales problems are thinking problems. The root cause is usually a CVS (about the customer, the product, or the process) that needs escaping — not a tactic that needs optimising.

## CPV — Customer Point of View

**Core principle:** CPV is cvs2bvs applied outward — escaping your internal view to genuinely enter the customer's world.

**Use when:** Product development, service design, customer experience, marketing strategy, retention issues.

### The 5 CPV Principles
1. **You ought to be able to see the customer's point of view whether or not you agree with it.** (The prerequisite — agreement is the unconscious filter. Principle 1 strips it out entirely. All other principles are downstream of this one.)
2. The customer's CVS is always different from yours — often more than you expect.
3. Evidence-based insight (data and observed behaviour) over internal assumption.
4. Every insight connects to an actionable recommendation and validation plan.
5. The BVS being offered must be visibly and significantly better than the customer's current CVS.

**The most important principle:** Principle 1. The word *ought* makes this a cognitive obligation, not a suggestion. This is cvs2bvs in its most demanding form: escaping not just your current view, but your *preference* for your current view.

### Three Customer Perspective Layers
- **Rational** — what the customer thinks (explicit goals, conscious needs)
- **Emotional** — what the customer feels (fears, desires, confidence levels)
- **Social** — what influences the customer (peers, credibility, identity)

**Framework:**
```
CUSTOMER PROFILE & CONTEXT:
- Target Customer Segment (be specific — different segments need separate CPVs)
- Current Situation (from customer POV)
- Customer Goals
- Pain Points

CUSTOMER JOURNEY ANALYSIS (5-stage):
1. Awareness Stage
2. Consideration Stage
3. Decision Stage
4. Experience Stage
5. Advocacy Stage

THREE PERSPECTIVE LAYERS per stage:
- Rational (think): explicit goals, conscious reasoning
- Emotional (feel): fears, desires, confidence, frustration
- Social (influenced by): peers, credibility signals, identity

CVS2BVS for the Customer:
- What does the customer's CVS look like right now?
- What BVS are we offering them?
- Is our BVS 10× better than their current CVS?
- What is the highest-risk moment in the journey (delight → friction point)?

STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS:
- 3 actions to better serve customer POV
- Each recommendation with a validation plan
```

**Key principle:** The customer's CVS is your starting point, not your product features. Enter their world first.

**CPV insight patterns (from real sessions):**
- The customer's BVS is almost never the product itself — it is what the product enables (e.g., Canva's BVS for non-designers = **confidence**, not design capability)
- The highest-risk journey moment is often the paywall or the delivery gap (where delight meets friction)
- The strongest CPV identifies how the customer becomes an involuntary distribution channel — the WOMBAT mechanic embedded in the product
- Segment differentiation matters: a single CPV for all customers produces generic insights. Separate CPVs per segment (e.g., solopreneur / SMB / enterprise / government) surface the real differences.
- The gap between what a product delivers and what a customer truly needs is the SOTlobe positioning opportunity.

### Case Study: Canva x SOTlobe (2026-05-03)
- **Customer CVS:** "I need to make this graphic look 2x better."
- **SOTlobe BVS:** "I need to make the thinking behind this graphic 10x more valuable."
- **Key Move:** Integrating GBB and DVR directly into the creation workflow so users become "x10 Strategists," not just "Template Users."

## DVR — Dollarization, Valuation, Return

**Core equation:**
```
(Expected Return - Investment Cost) / Investment Cost = ROI%

D = DOLLARIZATION (Translate benefits to financial dollar impact)
V = VALUATION (Extend to asset value impact)
R = RETURN (Map the virtuous investment cycle)
```

**Use when:** Evaluating investments, career decisions, technology adoption, B2B value selling, any go/no-go economic analysis.

### The Galileo Principle
*"Measure what can be measured, and make measurable what cannot be measured."*

This is the DVR mandate. Every benefit, without exception, can be expressed in dollars or measurable impact.

### D — Dollarization (detailed)
Price is what you pay. Cost is what you experience. They routinely point in opposite directions.

Six steps to dollarize any benefit:
1. Identify the alternative (what does the client do without this?)
2. State the benefit clearly
3. Quantify the improvement
4. Convert to dollars
5. Express per unit (per seat / per employee / per year)
6. Reveal the true net cost (total cost minus dollarized savings)

**Key insight:** When sellers can't dollarize, customers default to price comparison — and the better product loses.

### V — Valuation (detailed)
Extension of dollarization into asset impact.
- After dollarizing: what has happened to the valuation of the asset?
- What is the added x10 value for future cash flows?
- For start-ups: potential for growth and eventual profits
- Harder to calculate than dollarization — but the extra effort is worth it

**ROP → Shareholder Value chain:**
Better-thinking teams → higher earnings → lower execution risk → higher market multiple → higher shareholder value.

### R — Return (detailed)
The gain from an investment of time, energy, or capital.
- Virtuous cycle: INVESTMENT → RETURN → INVESTMENT
- Better return → better next round of investment
- DVRx10 sheet: map how return can be 10× the investment
- Applies to time and energy — 1 hour doing a DVR, dollarized return justifies more DVRs

### DVR Principles
1. You ought to be able to see the dollar value of any x10 thinking or selling application.
2. Be able to spell out the difference between ROI and asset valuation.
3. Customers buy only if perceived dollar value > price in dollars.
4. A DVRx10 sheet maps how return can be 10× the investment.
5. DVR is difficult at first — most don't try. It's a skill: the more DVRs you do, the easier it becomes.

### TUH DVR Audit (30-question diagnostic)
Three dimensions — 10 questions each:
- **Objectives:** clarify ideal outcomes, what is being improved, why the engagement is happening, operational and reputational impact, competitive gain
- **Metrics:** how success is measured, indicators, reporting frequency, progress standards per conversation, the "tripped over it" test
- **Value:** ROI/ROP/ROM assessment, bottom-line impact, annualised savings, intangible value, scope of impact, consequences of failure

Purpose: moves a client from vague intent to dollarized accountability — not just "what do you want?" but "how will you measure it, and what is it worth if you get it?"

### Nine Dollarization Entry Points (same product, nine client conversations)
A single offer can be dollarized from nine distinct pain points:
1. **Return on Payroll (ROP)** — one better decision/week per 10 people = $2.6M/yr
2. **Decision Cost Avoidance** — one poor strategic decision costs $50K–$500K; GBB halves reversal rate
3. **Sales Margin Expansion** — recovering 10% margin on $10M revenue = $1M/yr
4. **Meeting Cost Reduction** — 2 hrs/week reclaimed for team of 8 = $80K–$200K/yr
5. **Innovation Pipeline** — one new product/market entry = $500K+ year-one revenue
6. **Talent Retention** — 2 retained employees at $120K each = $120K–$480K avoided costs
7. **Customer Complaint Reduction** — 20% fewer complaints = $60K–$480K/yr
8. **Onboarding Acceleration** — 30% faster for 10 new hires/yr = $240K recovered output
9. **Crisis Cost Avoidance** — 1 crisis avoided per 3 years at $1M average = $333K/yr amortised

*Key insight: Client chooses whichever pain point hurts most. The seller needs all nine.*

### DVRx10 Response Format
```
SITUATION ANALYSIS: [Define the decision/investment]

D — DOLLARIZATION (10 points):
1. [Benefit label]: $X value derived as follows...
...

V — VALUATION (10 points):
1. [Asset impact]: $X or measurable effect...
...

R — RETURN (10 points):
1. Year 1: $X investment → $X return = Xx ROI
...

DVRx10 VERDICT: [Net cost of NOT investing > price of investing? Go/No-Go/Modify]
```

**Key principle:** Without dollarization, decisions are made on emotion or politics, not value. DVR is not just a sales tool — it is a capital allocation framework.

## EFU — Escape from Uncheck

**Core principle:** Most decisions are constrained by unchecked assumptions. EFU surfaces them, fact-checks them, and unleashes new possibilities.

**Use when:** Strategic planning; innovation projects; market entry; when traditional thinking hasn't worked.

**Format:**
```
SITUATION DEFINITION:
- Current Challenge
- Desired Outcome
- Assumed Constraints

E — EXPOSE UNCHECKED ASSUMPTIONS:
1. Process Assumptions ("We've always done it this way...")
2. Resource Assumptions ("We don't have enough...")
3. Market Assumptions ("Customers won't pay for...")
4. Timing Assumptions ("It's too early/late for...")
5. Competitive Assumptions ("The market is already taken...")

F — FACT-CHECK EACH ASSUMPTION:
- Which assumptions are actually true?
- Which are outdated CVS?
- Which are fears, not facts?

U — UNLEASH NEW POSSIBILITIES (BVS):
- With those assumptions removed: what becomes possible?
- 10 ideas that were previously "impossible"
```

## FTI — Fire This Idea

**Core structure:**
```
F — Fire Up Ideas (rapid generation)
T — Test Fire Power (rapid evaluation)
I — Ignite Acceleration (implementation)
```

**Use when:** Breaking creative blocks; rapid innovation; product ideation; accelerating concept to action.

**Format:**
```
INNOVATION CHALLENGE: [state clearly]

F — FIRE UP (Generate 10+ ideas rapidly):
- No filtering during generation
- Wild ideas welcome — EFU applies here
- Use x10 thinking to escape obvious solutions

T — TEST FIRE POWER (Evaluate top 3):
- Impact potential (1–10)
- Feasibility now (1–10)
- x10 multiplier effect (1–10)

I — IGNITE (For the top idea):
- Next 3 concrete actions
- First milestone (30 days)
- Success metric
```

## HWS — WOMBAT Selling

**WOMBAT = Word Of Mouth Buy And Tell**

**Use when:** Building referral-based growth; shifting from transactional to relationship selling; measuring customer advocacy.

**Framework:**

**Step 1: Establish the Three Profit Streams**
Track % of revenue from: WOMBATs / Referrals / Repeats

**Step 2: Implement WOMBAT Questions (OldSell → NewSell)**
| OldSell Question | NewSell Question |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| "Did you close the sale?" | "Did they replicate?" |
| "What's your conversion rate?" | "What's your WOMBAT rate?" |
| "How many new clients?" | "How many clients came from clients?" |

**Step 3: WOMBAT Audit**
- Name each WOMBAT individually
- Map their genealogy (who came from whom)
- Project 3-year lifetime value per WOMBAT chain
- Run EFUx10: what assumptions are limiting replication?

**10 WOMBAT KPIs:** ACQUIRE (−45% cost) / PRICE (−28% sensitivity) / SPEND (+47%) / SPEED (2–3×) / INTEREST (+37%) / REPEAT (2×) / REPLICATE (+49%) / VALUE (+72% lifetime) / TRUST (no defection)

## RPV — Replicator Point of View

**Core concept:** View situations from the perspective of WHAT WANTS TO REPLICATE ITSELF. Think like a meme seeking to spread from brain to brain.

**Three Replicator Power Traits:**
| Trait | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Fidelity** | Creates true, faithful copies — clear, precise, reliable |
| **Fecundity** | Abundant spreading potential — can replicate many times |
| **Longevity** | Lasting durability — sustains over time |

**Use when:** Designing viral growth; franchise development; scaling; products that spread naturally.

**Framework (3 steps):**

**Step 1: Identify the Replicator**
- What specific idea, behaviour, or transaction wants to spread?
- Personify it: "This idea wants to replicate because..."
- Identify the core value proposition that drives natural sharing

**Step 2: Test Replicator Power**
- Score on Fidelity / Fecundity / Longevity (1–10 each)
- Identify what's blocking replication (EFU applies)

**Step 3: Amplify and Deploy**
- Remove friction from the replication path
- Design triggers that prompt sharing
- Measure WOMBAT rate quarterly

## TPF — Ten Possible Futures

**Core concept:** A "brain app" for the PFC. Escape linear, single-track thinking by generating 3–10 distinct possible futures before deciding.

**Use when:** Strategic decisions with long-term consequences; major life decisions; risk assessment; scenario planning.

**Framework (3 steps):**

**Step 1: Define Current State and Decision Point**
- Articulate CVS clearly
- Identify time horizons: immediate (1yr) / medium-term (5yr) / long-term (25yr+)
- Acknowledge your CVS as one view, not the only view

**Step 2: Generate 10 Possible Futures (distinct scenarios)**
- Make each scenario DISTINCT — not variations of the same theme
- Include: optimistic / pessimistic / disruption / unexpected / slow burn / acceleration
- Name each scenario (e.g., "AI Partner", "Full Pivot", "Niche Specialist")

**Step 3: Analyse and Choose**
```
For each scenario:
- Probability (1–10)
- Impact if it occurs (1–10)
- What signals would indicate this future is arriving?
- What actions now would serve this future?

Recommendation: Which 2–3 futures to prepare for, and why.
```

## x10_selling — 10x Sales Multiplication

**Core equation:** cvs × 10 = bvs (Current Sales View × 10 = Better Sales View)

**Use when:** Transforming incremental (10%) into exponential (10x) sales growth; building sustainable revenue through customer replication.

**Core concept:** Stop chasing individual transactions. Build WOMBAT systems that replicate customers exponentially.

**Framework (3 steps):**

**Step 1: Establish the Three Revenue Streams**
Audit current revenue split:
- WOMBATs (replicated customers) — typically underutilised
- Referrals (direct referrals) — underutilised
- Repeats (existing customer return) — the foundation

**Step 2: x10 Challenge**
```
Current CVS (Sales):
- Revenue: $X
- Customer acquisition cost: $X
- Conversion rate: X%

x10 BVS Target:
- WOMBAT rate: X%
- Revenue from replication: $X
- Acquisition cost via WOMBATs: $X (−45%)

x10 Actions (10 breakthrough moves):
1. [Action]
... (10 total)
```

**Step 3: WOMBAT Infrastructure**
- Train team on NewSell questions
- Implement WOMBAT tracking
- Run quarterly WOMBAT audit
- Set CEO-level WOMBAT KPIs
